# Chicago Like a Local: Things to Do During DjangoCon US 2025 (Part 1)

DjangoCon US 2025 is coming to Chicago, and while you’ll be busy soaking in all things Django, there’s no reason you can’t experience the city like a local while you’re here.

Whether you’re looking for great food, cultural spots, or just a cool place to unwind, here are some top recommendations from a Chicago native.

_This is the first in a series of posts leading up to the conference—stay tuned for more Chicago insights as we get closer to the big week._

---

## Eats & Drinks

### Classic Chicago Bites

**Deep Dish Pizza**  
Tourists flock to [**Giordano’s**](https://giordanos.com), but locals will tell you to check out [**Pequod’s**](https://pequodspizza.com) for its caramelized crust or [**Bartoli’s**](https://www.bartolispizzeria.com) for a perfect balance of sauce and cheese.

**Italian Beef**  
Try [**Johnnie’s Beef**](https://www.johnniesbeef.com) in Elmwood Park if you’re willing to venture out, or [**Al’s Beef**](https://www.alsbeef.com) for a solid in-city option.

**Chicago-Style Hot Dog**  
No ketchup! Head to [**Portillo’s**](https://www.portillos.com) or [**Superdawg**](https://superdawg.com) for an iconic experience.

---

### Coffee & Work Spots

Need a quiet spot to get some work done or debrief from the conference?

- [**Intelligentsia Coffee**](https://www.intelligentsia.com) (Loop & Logan Square) – The OG of Chicago’s coffee scene.
- [**Metric Coffee**](https://metriccoffee.com) (West Loop) – Fantastic coffee and a great place to recharge.
- [**Chicago Roastery**](https://www.starbucksreserve.com/en-us/locations/chicago) (Loop) – A spectacular destination.

---

### After-Hours Hangouts

- [**Green Mill Jazz Club**](https://greenmilljazz.com) – One of the city’s oldest jazz bars, once frequented by Al Capone.

---

## Explore the City

### Cultural & Art Spots

- [**The Art Institute of Chicago**](https://www.artic.edu) – A must-visit for art lovers, home to classics like _A Sunday on La Grande Jatte_.
- [**Shedd Aquarium**](https://www.sheddaquarium.org) – A must-visit for marine life enthusiasts, featuring incredible exhibits and an oceanarium.

---

### Outdoor & Scenic Views

- [**The Riverwalk**](https://www.chicagoriverwalk.us) – Walk along the Chicago River for amazing skyline views and great people-watching.
- [**The 606**](https://www.the606.org) – A converted rail line turned walking/biking trail with art installations along the way.
- [**Lakefront Trail**](https://www.chicagoparkdistrict.com/parks-facilities/lakefront-trail) – Perfect for a morning jog with breathtaking views of Lake Michigan.

---

### Neighborhood Vibes

- [**Pilsen**](https://www.choosechicago.com/neighborhoods/pilsen/) – A vibrant neighborhood with incredible murals, art galleries, and some of the best Mexican food in the city.
- [**Andersonville**](https://www.andersonville.org) – Known for its charming local shops and Swedish heritage.

---

## Final Tips

**Public Transit**  
The CTA (L trains and buses) is your best friend. A Ventra card or contactless payment makes getting around easy.

**Weather Check**  
October can be unpredictable—bring layers!

**Hidden Gems**  
Ask locals! Chicagoans love sharing their favorite spots.

---

DjangoCon US 2025 is going to be an amazing event, and hopefully this guide helps you make the most of your time in Chicago. If you have extra time before or after the conference, explore beyond downtown—you won’t regret it.
